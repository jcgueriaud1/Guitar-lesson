Lesson 4 · Foundation

First Fingerpicking

Meet your picking hand — on the chords for Fast Car.

Why now, and why these chords. Your target song, Fast Car, is fingerpicked — so before the famous riff (next lesson), you need the picking hand working on its own. Good news: it uses four easy chords you nearly know — Cmaj7, G, Em, D — so we build the hand on the actual song's chords. One thing this lesson: a steady, even arpeggio. No capo needed yet.

Your picking hand

Each finger owns its own strings — learn this once:
  1. Thumb (p) → the bass strings (low E, A, D). It plays the chord's root and keeps the pulse.
  2. Index (i) → the G string.
  3. Middle (m) → the B string.
The pattern we'll drill is p – i – m – i (thumb, index, middle, index), over and over. Thumb steady underneath; fingers light on top. Full hand setup: reference/fingerpicking-hand.html.

Cmaj7 = your C shape with the index lifted (B string rings open). The thumb's bass note differs per chord — the trainer shows it.

The trainer — follow the plucks (≈8 min)

Em

The glowing string + letter shows which string to pluck and with which finger (p/i/m). Go slow enough that every note rings clean. Watch the thumb keep the beat.

Consistency over speed — always. A slow, even arpeggio where every note rings is the whole skill. Tense, rushed plucking builds nothing. Stay slow until it's automatic, then creep the tempo up.

How to practise

  1. Start on One chord (Em) at the slowest tempo. Just get p‑i‑m‑i even and clean, eyes on the picking hand.
  2. Try Cmaj7, then G and D the same way — notice the thumb moves to a different bass string for each.
  3. Switch to Full loop and let it change chords for you. Keep the picking pattern going while your fretting hand changes — exactly the “keep moving” idea from Lesson 2, now for the picking hand.
Ask your teacher. Thumb hitting the wrong string? Notes muted or buzzy? Hand cramping? Tell me. When p‑i‑m‑i flows around the full loop without stopping, you're ready for Lesson 5 — the actual Fast Car riff.
