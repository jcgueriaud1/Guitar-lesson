Lesson 3 · Your first song

Knockin' on Heaven's Door

Bob Dylan · four chords you already know, start to finish.

Why this song, now. It's the moment your drills become music. Just four chords — G, D, Am, C — at a slow, forgiving tempo, looping the same pattern the whole way through. It puts your G⇄D and C⇄G changes (Lesson 1) and your steady strum (Lesson 2) to work in a real, recognisable song. Get this start to finish and you've proven the foundation holds.

Hit Start. The chord changes for you — your only job is to strum it and be ready when “get ready →” lights up.

Start slower than feels necessary. 50 bpm, down-strums only, and just loop the four bars until the changes happen without you thinking. Speed and Old Faithful come after that — there's no prize for rushing.

How to practise it

  1. Loop the player at 50 bpm, one down-strum per beat. The 2-beat G and D just get two downs each; Am and C get four.
  2. When that's smooth, nudge the tempo up toward ~70 bpm (the record's pace), or switch to Old Faithful on the full-bar chords.
  3. Finally, put on the actual recording and play along — that's the real win, and where the rhythm locks in.
Ask your teacher. Is the G→D in two beats rushing you? Which strum to use on the half-bars? Want the optional Dylan embellishments (Am→Am7, C→Cadd9 — both just add your pinky)? Tell me. When you can loop the whole thing without stopping, send word — that's a real milestone, and Lesson 4 opens up your first fingerpicking.
