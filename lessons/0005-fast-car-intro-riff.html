Lesson 5 · The song

Fast Car — the intro riff

Tracy Chapman · the fingerpicked loop that runs the whole song.

🎸 Capo on fret 2
Now you put it together. Fast Car is built on one fingerpicked loop — Cmaj7 → G → Em → D, repeated — and you now have the picking hand (Lesson 4) and the chords. This lesson rolls them into the song's signature arpeggio. It's a step up, so the plan is the same as always: dead slow first, tempo later. Chords, capo & structure verified: JustinGuitar · Ultimate Guitar.

Thumb on the bass, then roll up the strings with i‑m‑a and back. Let the chord changes happen while the picking keeps rolling.

Straight talk about this trainer. What it plays is a clean rolling arpeggio over Fast Car's real chords — it builds the exact right-hand motion and the feel of the song. The iconic recorded intro adds a few specific melody notes and a signature hammer‑on/pull‑off on top. Once this arpeggio flows, learn those note‑for‑note from the full tab: JustinGuitar lesson · Songsterr (with playback).
The song is ~104 bpm — don't start there. Begin painfully slow, get the arpeggio even and every note ringing, and let speed arrive on its own. Rushing a riff this exposed just teaches the mistakes.

How to practise

  1. No capo, one chord first if it's shaky — go back to Lesson 4 and get p‑i‑m‑i effortless, then come here.
  2. Capo on, slow loop. Run the trainer at the slowest tempo, just Cmaj7→G→Em→D, picking hand rolling, fretting hand changing underneath.
  3. Creep the tempo up a little at a time. When it's smooth, open the full tab and add the melody notes on top.
  4. Play to the record — the real milestone.
Ask your teacher. Capo buzzing the strings? Arpeggio uneven? Want me to break the exact recorded riff into its own micro-lesson with the melody notes? Just say so — this is a big one and worth taking in pieces.
