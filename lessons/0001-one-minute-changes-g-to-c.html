Lesson 1 · Foundation

One Minute Changes: G ⇄ C

Make one chord change clean, fast, and automatic.

Why this, why first. You already know the shapes — your bottleneck is the change. G⇄C is the change to own first: it's everywhere in beginner songs, and it forces a big hand reposition, so cracking it makes the others feel easy. This is the foundation the whole mission stands on.

The two shapes

G major
C major

● = finger, number = which finger · ○ = open string · ✕ = don't play

The one idea: minimal movement

  1. Find the pivot. Going G→C, your finger 1 only slides a little and your hand shape barely opens. Don't lift the whole hand off the neck — move as little as possible.
  2. Move as a unit. Try to land all fingers together, not one at a time. Aim them at the new shape before they touch down.
  3. Air changes. Lift just off the strings and switch shapes in the air, over and over, with no pressing. Train the shape first, the pressing second.
  4. Clean > fast. Press, strum once, check every note rings. Only speed up when it's clean. Never drill a buzz.

Best pace held clean: —

Start at 30 — your current pace. When you can hit ~10 clean changes in a row at a setting, nudge it up +2 and tap “held it” to save the new best. Drop back the moment notes buzz. Clean always wins over fast. Rest your hand between rounds.

Make it music (≈4 min)

Count a slow 1–2–3–4 out loud. Strum G once on beat 1, let beats 2–3–4 ring while you get your fingers to C, strum C on the next beat 1. The goal isn't speed — it's changing in time without stopping the count. If you have to pause, slow the count down until you don't.
