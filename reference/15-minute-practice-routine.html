The 15-Minute Practice Routine

A template you can run on any day, with whatever chords or song you're working on. Little and often beats long and rare. Missing a day is fine — just come back.

0–2 min

Loosen up

Form each chord you'll use today, one at a time. Press, check every note rings clean, release. No rush — this wakes the hand.

2–9 min

The drill (the real work)

Whatever this week's lesson asks — usually One Minute Changes on a chord pair. This is where the foundation is built. Stay here the longest.

9–14 min

Make it music

Apply the drill to a real, slow musical context: a 4-beat strum, a song snippet, the chords in a loop. Slow and clean beats fast and messy — always.

14–15 min

Log it

One line: what you did, best score, how it felt. This is how your teacher calibrates the next lesson. Tell me in chat, or jot it.

The golden rule: never practise a mistake up to speed. If a change is messy, slow down until it's clean, then let it speed up on its own. Clean reps are the only reps that count.
