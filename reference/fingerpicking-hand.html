The Fingerpicking Hand

The classical naming (p, i, m, a) and which finger owns which string. Learn these assignments once and every pattern gets easier.

FingerSymbolPlays
ThumbpThe bass strings — low E (6), A (5), D (4). Picks the root note of the chord.
IndexiThe G string (3).
MiddlemThe B string (2).
RingaThe high e string (1).

Hand setup

  • Thumb stays out front of the fingers, not tucked behind them — it moves down across the bass strings.
  • Fingers pluck up and slightly in, toward your palm — a small, relaxed motion, not a big yank.
  • Anchor lightly or float. Some rest the pinky near the soundhole for stability; either is fine — whatever keeps the hand relaxed and consistent.
  • Let the bass ring. The thumb keeps a steady pulse underneath while the fingers add the higher notes on top.
The whole game is consistency. Slow, even, relaxed plucks beat fast and tense every time. The thumb is your metronome — keep it steady and the fingers will follow.
