# G⇄C change ≈ 30/min, paced beat works better than self-tally

As of 2026-06-08, the user can make roughly **30 clean G⇄C changes per minute**
— one every ~2 seconds. That's about halfway to the ~60/min fluency target, so
the chord-change foundation is partway built, not absent. Teaching should push
the *pace* up gradually from 30, not start from zero.

**Method insight:** the self-tally feedback loop (tap +1 per change) failed in
practice — you can't strum and tap at the same time. A **hands-free** loop works
far better: a metronome beeps the target pace and the user simply lands the chord
on each beep. Default future drills to a paced beat at the user's current rate,
nudged up over days, rather than counting.

Implications: Lesson 1 rebuilt around a metronome pace trainer (start 30 → climb).
Lesson 2 (changes in time with a steady strum) is well within reach once 30/min
feels comfortable.
