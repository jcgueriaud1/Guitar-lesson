# Mission: Acoustic Fingerstyle & Strumming Guitar

## Why
I want to play acoustic guitar fluidly with my fingers — both strumming and
fingerpicking — and to move between the two *within* a song without stopping.
The path there is a steady diet of songs, easy to advanced, that quietly drill
the technique. Foundations matter more to me than speed; I'd rather go slow and
build it right.

## Success looks like
- I can change between common open chords (G, C, D, Em, Am, etc.) cleanly and in
  time, without looking and without pausing the rhythm.
- I can strum a song with a steady, musical rhythm.
- I can fingerpick a song using basic Travis picking (alternating-bass thumb +
  fingers on the treble strings).
- I can switch from strumming to fingerpicking and back inside one song.
- I have a growing repertoire of songs I can actually play start to finish.

## Constraints
- **~15 minutes per day, maximum.** Lessons and routines must fit this. Little
  and often beats long and rare.
- Acoustic guitar, played fingerstyle (no pick — or pick optional, but the goal
  is fingers).
- I already know the basic open chords; my limitation is *changing* between them
  smoothly, not forming them.
- I don't want to rush. Solid foundations first.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Reading standard notation / sheet music (tabs and chord boxes are fine).
- Music theory beyond what a given song needs.
- Soloing, lead guitar, scales for improvisation.
- Barre chords — until open-chord changes and basic fingerpicking are solid.
